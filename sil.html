MATH 597, Section 004: An introduction to Alexandrov geometry

Alexandrov spaces are defined via axioms similar to those given by Euclid. The Alexandrov axioms replace certain equalities with inequalities. Depending on the signs of the inequalities, we obtain Alexandrov spaces with curvature bounded above and curvature bounded below. The definitions of the two classes of spaces are similar, but their properties and known applications are quite different.

We will follow the historical development of this subject with emphasize on applications.

Topics for curvature bounded below should include Alexandrov embedding theorem, Bishop--Gromov inequality and Gromov's compactness theorem, globalization theorem, Toponogov splitting theorem, finiteness theorems, Hsiang--Kleiner theorem.

Topics for curvature bounded above should include Reshetnyak's majorization theorem, Reshetnyak's gluing theorem, Hadamrd--Cartan theorem, Burago--Ferleger--Kononenko collision theorem, Davis' construction of exotic aspherical spaces.
